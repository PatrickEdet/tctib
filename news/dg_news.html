HIGHLIGHTS OF INTERACTIVE SESSION WITH THE MANAGEMENT OF TEACHERS REGISTRATION COUNCIL OF NIGERIA.TRCN. ABUJA

BY Prof Taoheed Adedoja Director General Teachers Continuous Training Institute, TCTI Biase, Cross River State.
TCTI SETS A STATE-OF-THE-ART DIGITAL TRAINING PLATFORM AND BECOMES THE 1st PUBLIC INSTITUTION WITH CERTIPORT AUTHORIZED TESTING CENTRE (CATC) FOR DIGITAL LITERACY AND EMERGING SKILLS TRAINING AND CERTIFICATION.

TCTI,established in 2020 is located in a serene environment of Biase in Cross River State, standing tall as one of the major 21st century initiatives of His Excellency, the then Governor of Cross River State, Sen. Prof Ben Ayade to proffer solution to inadequacies in human capacity development that has hindered Nigeria’s growth especially, the education sector.

In line with the vision, mission, and objectives of the Teachers Continuous Training Institute (TCTI) to provide trending 21st century retraining, the Management of TCTI, Biase with the then Governor, Professor Ben Ayade as the initiator and visioner, boasts of a State-of-the-Art Digital compliant Training Centre, a Demonstration School, activated TCTI Certiport Authorized Testing Center and other world class facilities that put TCTI Biase at the lead, setting pace for other public institutions and defining what training institution of 21st century should be and deliver.

With the blend of best of both digital and human resources, TCTI has enormous capability to render relevant and standard human capital programmes as the Institute sets out to address the urgent need for re-skilling, retooling, re-imagining and rethinking today’s knowledge systems in the fast-paced technology driven world as educators face growing challenges as they teach a very “wired” and more and more “wireless” generation of students using technology that is evolving every day.

Without any doubt, Nigerian teachers require far broader range of skills and attributes than the technical capability or competence that career practices formerly demanded. Teachers’ continuous development and practices need to catch up with systematic reforms evidently propelled by TRCN under Prof. Josiah Ajiboye in the last 5 years, if only to drive desired transformation in teacher’s capability to prime the pump of learning.

Incorporation of digital literacy into the human capital development agenda of TCTI Biase is one of those add-ons to TRCN reforms and a deliverable which we strongly belief would stimulate tremendous growth, sustainable development and establish every Nigerian teacher as ‘Inspector Gadget’ – one who can continuously adapt, learn and grow in a fast-changing world and be able to apply a depth of skill to a progressively widening scope of situations and experiences, equally at ease with technical issues as with subject matter.
